What's on the menu, QUEENSTOWN

Cross-Country, Trail, Freeride, Downhill

Not only for the Gravity Riders...

There’s been a lot of mountain biking news out of Queenstown recently about the gondola development. Riders could be forgiven in thinking that Queenstown mainly caters to the gravity riders. leaving lovers of pedalling uphill or buggering off into the backcountry out in the cold (metaphorically).

Recent trail developments by the Queenstown Mountain Bike Club are a nice reminder that Queenstown has a full menu of riding for the discerning mountain biker. The shiny new Rude Rock Trail connects Coronet Peak with the Skippers Canyon and Zoot Track trails. Coronet Peak no longer offers chairlift access for mountain bikes but this hasn’t stopped riders from riding up to enjoy the trails. The old Brake Burner course is a particular favourite for locals and visitors alike.

Rude Rock!

Rude Rock Trail offers a beautifully built singletrack link between Coronet Peak and Skippers Saddle; with spectacular scenery, alpine elevation, natural flow, buff berms and a somewhat phallic landmark that gives the trail it’s name. The 3km trail traverses Department of Conservation land, requiring a lot of groundwork before the digging could begin. At over 1000 metres above sea level tussock removal is an issue and a detailed surveying/ consent process needed to happen first. All up the trail took a year from concept to completion, with $10,000 funding coming from the Queenstown Trail Trust. Work was carried out by Extra Mile Trail Building using a small digger and hand tools.

The Coronet Peak area isn’t the only place to receive new trail loving in Queenstown. The 2.5km Gold Digger Trail off Moke Lake Road was finished late 2012, adding quality singletrack to the Moonlight/ Moke Lake Loop. As the name suggests Gold Digger travels through historic gold mining works as it crosses back and forth over 7 Mile Creek. Queenstown Mountain Bike Club is working towards a big picture view of riding in their mountain paradise. All of this trail development is gradually ticking off a wishlist of interconnected trails to bring longer and more varied trail riding to the area. Cross country, epic, trail riding - whatever you call it; there’s a whole lot more of it in Queenstown these days. Excellent.
